Internal Lending Policy (Prototype)
=====================================
Simulated Retail Lending Policy — Personal & Home Loans
Indian Banking Context | Academic Prototype


CREDIT SCORE RULES
-------------------
- 750 and above: Strong profile. Eligible for best available interest rate. Fast-track approval for standard loan amounts.
- 700–749: Good profile. Eligible for approval with standard processing. Minor supporting documents may be requested.
- 650–699: Moderate risk. Mandatory manual review. Approval possible with co-applicant, collateral, or reduced loan amount.
- 600–649: High risk. Approval only under exceptional circumstances with strong compensating factors (e.g., large down payment, high income, existing relationship with bank).
- Below 600: Reject. Application does not meet minimum creditworthiness threshold.
- No credit history (NTC): Route to manual review. Evaluate based on income stability, banking behavior, and employment profile.


INCOME RULES
-------------
Personal Loans:
- Minimum net monthly income: ₹20,000 for salaried applicants in Tier-2/3 cities.
- Minimum net monthly income: ₹30,000 for salaried applicants in metro cities.
- Salary must be received via bank transfer (not cash) for at least the last 3 months.
- Salary account must show consistent credits — irregular or declining salary patterns trigger manual review.
- At least 6 months of continuous employment with current employer is required.

Home Loans:
- Minimum net monthly income: ₹35,000 for Tier-2/3 cities; ₹50,000 for metros.
- Co-applicant income can be clubbed if the co-applicant is a spouse, parent, or earning child.

Self-Employed Applicants:
- Minimum 2 years of ITR (Income Tax Returns) filing required.
- Business vintage of at least 3 years preferred; minimum 2 years acceptable.
- Latest 2 years of audited financials or GST returns required for income verification.
- Income considered is average net profit over last 2 ITR years, not gross revenue.
- Irregular or significantly declining income over the 2-year period triggers high-risk flag.


DEBT BURDEN RULES
------------------
- FOIR (Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio) must not exceed 50% for salaried applicants.
- FOIR must not exceed 55% for self-employed applicants given income variability.
- FOIR calculation includes: all active EMIs + proposed new EMI, divided by net monthly income.
- Applicants with FOIR between 50–60% may be approved with a reduced loan amount that brings FOIR to 50% or below.
- Applicants with FOIR above 60% are rejected unless a strong co-applicant brings the blended FOIR below the threshold.
- More than 3 active open loans at time of application is a red flag. Route to manual review regardless of FOIR.
- Any EMI default in the past 12 months — even a single 30-day delay — is flagged and requires explanation.
- Credit card utilization consistently above 80% of limit is treated as a debt stress indicator.


LOAN AMOUNT RULES
------------------
Small Ticket (Personal Loans up to ₹2,00,000):
- Fast-track processing if credit score is 700+, FOIR is under 45%, and income is verified.
- Minimal documentation: last 3 months salary slips, 6-month bank statement, KYC.
- Decision target: 24–48 hours.

Medium Ticket (₹2,00,001 to ₹15,00,000):
- Standard review process. Full income and employment verification required.
- Bank statement analysis for last 12 months.
- Decision target: 3–5 working days.

High Ticket (Above ₹15,00,000 — including home loans):
- Enhanced due diligence. Physical or video verification of residence and employment may be required.
- Property legal and technical verification mandatory for home loans.
- Credit committee review for amounts above ₹50,00,000.
- Decision target: 7–15 working days.


EMPLOYMENT RULES
-----------------
Salaried Applicants:
- Permanent employees of central/state government, PSUs, and large private companies are lowest risk.
- Employees of registered SMEs are acceptable with slightly higher income thresholds.
- Contract, probationary, or gig workers require at least 12 months of documented stable income before eligibility.
- Minimum 6 months with current employer. For home loans, minimum 1 year preferred.
- If applicant has changed jobs in the last 6 months, continuity of income must be demonstrated across employers.

Self-Employed Applicants:
- Sole proprietors, partners, and private limited company directors are all eligible.
- Business must be registered and GST-compliant (if turnover exceeds threshold).
- Minimum 2 years business vintage for personal loans; 3 years for home loans.
- Seasonal businesses must show consistent annualized income across at least 2 filing years.


FRAUD & RED FLAG RULES
-----------------------
- Document mismatch: Name, address, or income figures inconsistent across PAN, Aadhaar, ITR, and bank statements — reject or escalate immediately.
- Loan application surge: More than 3 hard credit inquiries in the last 90 days from different lenders — high-risk flag, manual review required.
- Unverifiable income: Cash salary without bank evidence, inflated ITR with low banking activity, sudden income spike in the last filing year — reject or deep review.
- Address inconsistency: Residential address on application does not match KYC documents without a valid explanation — flag for verification.
- Profile mismatch: Stated occupation and income level inconsistent with declared lifestyle or loan purpose — flag for review.
- Negative banking behavior: Frequent overdrafts, returned cheques, or minimal savings despite stated income — treat as stress signal.
- Known NPA association: Applicant previously classified as Non-Performing Asset at any institution — automatic reject unless fully settled with NOC and 2-year clean history post-settlement.


FINAL DECISION MATRIX
----------------------
APPROVE:
- Credit score 700+, FOIR under 50%, verified income, clean repayment history, no red flags.

APPROVE WITH REDUCED AMOUNT:
- Credit score 650–699, or FOIR between 50–60%, or minor documentation gaps.
- Reduce loan amount to bring FOIR to 45–50% and re-evaluate.

MANUAL REVIEW:
- NTC (no credit history) applicants.
- Borderline scores (650–699) with mixed signals.
- Self-employed with complex income structure.
- Any red flag triggered that does not meet automatic reject criteria.
- Co-applicant cases where primary applicant does not independently qualify.

REJECT:
- Credit score below 600.
- FOIR above 60% with no co-applicant solution.
- Active NPA status or unsettled write-off history.
- Fraud or document falsification detected.
- Income entirely unverifiable.
- More than 3 active defaults in bureau report.


AI RECOMMENDATION NOTES
------------------------
1. Never generate an Approve decision when any fraud red flag is active — escalate to manual review regardless of credit score or income strength.
2. Always state the top 2–3 factors that drove the recommendation, so a human reviewer can quickly validate or override.
3. When FOIR is the main barrier, calculate and suggest the maximum loan amount that would bring FOIR to exactly 50% — do not just reject.
4. Treat NTC applicants as a separate path — do not score them the same way as applicants with a bureau history; use income and banking behavior as primary signals.
5. Do not approve high-ticket loans (above ₹15 lakh) based on AI output alone — always route to human review with your analysis as input.
6. Flag any case where income in bank statements and income in ITR differ by more than 30% — do not assume either figure is correct.
7. When co-applicant income is being clubbed, verify that the co-applicant independently meets minimum income criteria before including their income in FOIR calculations.
8. Confidence score must be included with every recommendation — low-confidence outputs (below 70%) must be automatically routed to manual review, not pushed to a decision.
